Letter to Rasulullah (P.B.U.H)
A reflection from the heart

Assalam O Alaikum Ya Rasulullah (P.B.U.H),

I know you are watching from above the seven skies. You might be disappointed in me, as I have not always lived by your teachings. Sometimes, I wish I could have been present when you were bringing faith, but then I fear: Would I have been among the sinners or those who did not believe in Allah?

Every lesson life taught me reminded me of your sayings that I once neglected.

"After me you will see rulers not giving you your right (but you should give them their right) and be patient till you meet me."
[Sahih al-Bukhari 7057]

But I did not remain patient. I questioned Allah for my losses, thinking I deserved better. I felt lost in this world of injustice, corruption, and moral decay where people have forgotten your teachings of honesty, compassion, and justice.

"If you were to rely upon Allah with reliance due to Him, He would provide for you just as He provides for the birds. They go out in the morning with empty stomachs and return full."
[Sunan al-Tirmidhi 2344]

My soul felt heavy with disappointment. I tried to cry but even my eyes seemed ashamed, refusing to weep. Overwhelmed, I closed my eyes and saw someone in sujood. When I opened them, your saying echoed in my ears:

"Allah is more pleased with the repentance of His slave than anyone of you is pleased with finding his camel which he had lost in the desert."
[Sahih Muslim 6309]

I rushed to make wuzu, my body trembling. I recited Surah Al-Fatiha and bowed down, crying until my soul felt free, as if Allah said: “I still love you and I am always here for you.”

That day I discovered life’s true meaning: to make Allah happy.

Ya Rasulullah (P.B.U.H), can someone miss a person they never met? Yes, I miss you deeply. When I see this world’s unfairness, I grow tired of fighting, but your sayings remind me that you are still with me.

When I learned that reciting Darood makes angels carry my name to you, I began reciting it often. Knowing that even once my name reaches you feels like the greatest reward.

Like everyone, I am busy in life, yet I try to make things right. I wait for my turn, I fear the grave, but I remind myself: “Trust the One who is the Most Rahman and Raheem.”

This gives me strength to continue struggling — not only to be a Muslim, but to become a true Momin.

Made with sincerity and reflection
